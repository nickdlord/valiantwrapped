Dr. Sajal Islam's research has evolved over time, focusing primarily on understanding and mitigating the effects of single-event burnout (SEB) in semiconductor devices under heavy-ion irradiation. Initially, his work centered on investigating SEB in gallium oxide (Ga2O3) and silicon carbide (SiC) devices, exploring the impact of device structure, epitaxial layer thickness, and doping concentration on SEB thresholds. As his research progressed, he expanded his scope to include the effects of heavy-ion irradiation on device degradation, examining the dependencies on device parameters and developing novel device designs to enhance resilience against SEB and single-event leakage current (SELC).

Dr. Islam's primary areas of expertise lie in the fields of radiation effects in semiconductors, device reliability, and physical modeling. His work has contributed significantly to the understanding of SEB mechanisms in various semiconductor materials and the development of predictive models for device degradation. He continues to investigate the effects of heavy-ion irradiation on semiconductor devices, aiming to optimize their performance and reliability in harsh environments.
