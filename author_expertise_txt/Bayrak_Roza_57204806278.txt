Roza Bayrak is a researcher with a focus on advancing the field of neuroimaging analysis, particularly in the areas of white matter analysis, physiological artifact removal and modeling, and machine learning and graph-based methods. Her early work focused on evaluating variability in white matter tractography and developing methods for characterizing and correcting signal drift in diffusion weighted MRI.

In recent years, her research has expanded to incorporate machine learning and graph-based approaches for analyzing brain connectomes and modeling physiological signals from functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data. She has explored the application of deep learning techniques for inferring respiratory and cardiac signals from fMRI dynamics, as well as developing methods for reconstructing physiological signals from fMRI across the adult lifespan. Her work aims to improve the accuracy and interpretability of neuroimaging data, ultimately contributing to a deeper understanding of brain function and behavior.
