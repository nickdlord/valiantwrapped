Anders Shilo's research focuses on human-centered design, clinical decision support, and the intersection of technology and healthcare. Initially, his work emphasized the importance of user-centered design in healthcare technology development, highlighting the need to involve end-users in the design process to improve usability and effectiveness. His early research explored the use of human factors and ergonomics in healthcare settings, examining topics such as patient safety, clinical decision-making, and the design of healthcare technologies.

Over time, Shilo's research evolved to incorporate emerging trends and technologies, including artificial intelligence and machine learning. He investigated the role and use of race in AI and ML models related to health, as well as the implementation of AI across healthcare settings. His work also delves into the challenges and opportunities presented by the increasing use of technology in healthcare, including the need for clinician education and training, the impact on clinician workflow and decision-making, and the potential for improved patient outcomes. Through his research, Shilo aims to advance the development of healthcare technologies that prioritize patient-centered care and support the well-being of both patients and healthcare professionals.
