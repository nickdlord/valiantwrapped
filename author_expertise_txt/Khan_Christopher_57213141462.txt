Christopher Khan is a researcher who has made significant contributions to the field of ultrasound imaging, particularly in the areas of deep learning and beamforming. His early work focused on developing deep learning methods for ultrasound beamforming and addressing the challenges associated with domain adaptation, as seen in his papers on domain adaptation for ultrasound beamforming (2020) and training deep network ultrasound beamformers with unlabeled in vivo data (2022). 

Over time, his research has evolved to emphasize improving ultrasound image quality through clutter reduction and exploring regularization techniques for deep learning models in ultrasound. He has investigated various approaches, including the use of generalized contrast-to-noise ratio regularization (GCNR) and learnable regularization via Padé activation units. His primary areas of expertise lie at the intersection of deep learning, ultrasound imaging, and beamforming, with a focus on developing efficient and accurate methods for ultrasound image reconstruction and analysis.
