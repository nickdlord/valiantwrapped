THEMES:
- **Medical Imaging and Diagnostics**:
- Application of deep learning techniques in medical imaging (Raman spectroscopy, histopathology)
- Development of new methods for image analysis (instance segmentation, registration, reconstruction)
- **Healthcare and Disease Surveillance**:
- Importance of large-scale epidemiological data for effective disease surveillance systems
- Use of distributed data for survival analysis and disease monitoring
- **Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning**:
- Utilization of foundation models in healthcare applications (kidney pathology, disease diagnosis)
- Application of ensemble learning and transfer learning in medical image analysis
- **Data Management and Sharing**:
- Challenges and solutions for efficient data sharing in medical image analysis (dataset distillation, pattern recognition)

SUMMARY:
Marilyn Lionts is a researcher with a multifaceted background in Medical Imaging and Diagnostics, Healthcare and Disease Surveillance, Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, and Data Management and Sharing. Her work has primarily focused on applying deep learning techniques to improve medical imaging outcomes, particularly in Raman spectroscopy and histopathology. She has also developed innovative methods for instance segmentation, registration, and reconstruction in medical images.

Over time, Marilyn's research has evolved to encompass various aspects of healthcare and disease surveillance, including the importance of large-scale epidemiological data for effective disease monitoring systems and the use of distributed data for survival analysis. Additionally, she has explored the application of artificial intelligence and machine learning in healthcare, leveraging foundation models in kidney pathology and disease diagnosis, as well as ensemble learning and transfer learning in medical image analysis. Her work also touches on the challenges and solutions for efficient data sharing in medical image analysis, such as dataset distillation and pattern recognition.
